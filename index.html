About Artist Zander

Jacob Alexander Figueroa, widely known as Zander, is an accomplished and visionary American artist celebrated for his innovative contributions to performance art, visual arts, music, and mixed media. Born on August 1, 1986, in Saint Paul, Minnesota, Zander’s work transcends traditional boundaries, blending conceptual art, expressionism, and surrealism into transformative experiences.

Zander's photography has been exhibited alongside the iconic works of Yoko Ono, cementing his place among globally renowned artists. His acclaimed exhibition, Translucent Threads, featured the celebrated artwork Path of Passion, and has been displayed at prestigious venues like the Broadway Gallery in New York City while earning coverage in NY Arts Magazine.

In addition to his visual art, Zander’s passion for storytelling extends to multimedia projects. He served as a consultant for Game Show Network’s Skin Wars, which is featured on Netflix and ran for three seasons. Zander’s influence also extends to the airwaves, with his music played alongside iconic bands such as Sonic Youth on public radio stations. He has been featured on CCTV, Minnesota Public Radio (MPR), and other platforms for his groundbreaking work.

Zander co-founded The Band Famous, a first-of-its-kind multimedia project that produced the world's first app album. As part of this venture, he operated a creative space in Los Angeles that functioned as both an art gallery and a live music venue, fostering a dynamic environment for collaboration, performances, and exhibitions. Additionally, Zander founded the Karnak Gallery, further solidifying his influence within the art world.

Zander’s philosophy centers around transformation, individuality, and connection. From collaborating with international acts like Paul Van Dyk and Benny Benassi in body painting projects to curating multi-sensory artistic events, Zander continues to redefine the boundaries of creativity, inspiring and empowering audiences around the globe.
